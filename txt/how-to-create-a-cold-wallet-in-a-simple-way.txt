How to create a Cold Wallet in a simple way
February 2026

Few weeks ago I created a cold wallet to protect my satoshis. So, I decided to document the process. It's important to note that this guide is for people who understand the basics of Bitcoin, this is why I'll not be extreme detailist, I'm assuming you know the concepts and best practices mentioned below.

=== Why a cold wallet? Why not a hot wallet? ===

First, study purposes. I wanted to learn how to create one and the concepts envolved. Second, security. If the wallet amount starts increase, at least it's following the best auto-custody practices.

Good, let's start. First, you need to setup a safe offline environment. Your seed should be generated in an environment that will never conect to the internet again.

=== Create the offline setup ===

For this, you will need a bootable pen-drive with Tails. Tails is an amnesic operational system (OS) focused on security. It boots from a pen-drive and all data is cleaned on shutdown.

[Install Tails](https://tails.net/) on a pen-drive with at least 16GB of storage. After that, configure your BIOS to boot from your pen-drive instead of the internal SSD or HD.

The wallet's seed will be generated in this first boot. Whenever you boot Tails, the startup will always be like a first setup, since the storage is cleaned on shutdown. So, just start system without any special configuration like "Persistable Storage". With Tails started, you will need to turn on the network (wired or Wi-Fi), this is the unique moment you will do it. Connect to our network, access https://github.com/iancoleman/bip39/releases, and download the "bip39-standalone.html" file. Then, turn off network.

=== Generate seed ===

Open the HTML file, you will see the "Mnemonic Code Converter" page. This is the page you will generate your wallet's private seed. Follow these steps:

1. Choose if it will have 12 or 24 words.
2. Mark "Show entropy details" checkbox. It will display a text area to input the entropy of your seed. Entropy is a way to make sure your seed will be truly random. It accepts either binary, base 6, 6-sided dice, base 10, hexadecimal or cards. Choose your preference and starts generating the entropy. For example, if you choose binary, you can use a coin to input entropy: head is 1, tail is 0. Make sure to input 256 bits of entropy.
3. Generate your passphrase. This part is optional, if you don't want this extra layer of security, you can skip this step.
4. Now, save your seed. It's the 12 words (or 24 depending on your choice) on "BIP39 Mnemonic" field. Save your seed offline. Don't screenshot, don't take photo, don't copy and paste. Write in a paper, but consider use a more advanced and protected way later, like write in metal.
5. Save your passphrase if you added one
6. Also, write the final characters of the first receiving address. It will be important in wallet restore step. To see your receiving addresses, go to"Derivation Path" section and select "BIP84". Then, scroll to "Derived Addresses" and check the "Address" column of the addresses table. Take the first one and write (or memorize) the final part, can be the 4 last characters (e.g. "kjh5").

With the seed, passphrase and address piece saved in safety. Shutdown the system.

=== Access your wallet ===

Boot Tails again. Don't turn on network again. You will never do it anymore.

Open Electrum wallet, this app is already instaled in Tails. You can open directly by clicking in the menu shortcut, but I recommend do it using terminal as you maybe will need to run a command before open Electrum.

Open terminal and run this command: "export QT_QPA_PLATFORM=xcb". It will force Electrum to use "xcb" plugin to run the app. It's a workaround for compatibility issues on Linux systems. After that, run "electrum". It will open the wallet app. Now, follow these steps:

1. Name your wallet.
2. Choose "Standalone wallet" as kind of wallet.
3. Choose the option "I already have a seed".
4. Input your seed.
5. Click "Options" and mark "BIP39" as seed type. If you created your seed with a passphrase, mark the "Extend this seed with custom words" checkbox.
6. Leave the type of addresses marked as "native segwit".
7. You can ignore the password step.
8. With the wallet opened, click on "View" menu, then click "Show addresses"

If you put all the information correctly, the first address of the receiving addresses list will match with the characters you noted before. Now, reboot Tails. Then follow the steps again to double check you saved all the data correctly.

=== Send BTC to your wallet ===

To do it you need to copy one of the receiving address of your wallet. Here you have two options: generate a QRCode (it's a feature of Electrum) or setup a Watch-Only wallet. I recommend setup Watch-Only with BlueWallet app. BlueWallet always give you a unused address for each transaction, which is a good practice.

To setup your Watch-Only wallet, [install BlueWallet](https://bluewallet.io/) in your smartphone and import your wallet by scanning the zpub key with the generated QRCode. This key is available on "Info" option under "Wallet" menu.

Once your Watch-Only wallet is created. Click on "Receive" button and copy the address. Use it to transfer your BTCs. For test and security purposes, transfer a small quantity. You can check the transaction status on BlueWallet. With the transaction confirmed, let's move to the final and most important step.

=== The final test! Very important! Don't skip ===

This is the full wallet restore step.

1. Delete your WatchOnly wallet from BlueWallet
2. Reboot Tails.
3. Follow the steps I mentioned above to access your wallet.
4. Check the addresses.
5. Use zpub key QRCode to import the Watch-Only wallet on BlueWallet again.

If everything is correct, you will be able to see your balance on the BlueWallet app.

=== Final advices ===

For legal and fiscal purposes don't mix BTC from different origins in the same wallet, like different exchanges, P2P, etc.
